# Architecture Review Board (ARB) Membership

## 1. Introduction

The Architecture Review Board (ARB) plays a critical role in shaping and maintaining the technical direction of the organization. This document outlines the structure, membership types, and requirements for participation in the ARB, ensuring a fair and transparent process for selecting members.

---

## 2. Membership Structure

### 2.1 Permanent Members
Permanent Members are core participants who provide consistent leadership and decision-making within the ARB. They are responsible for:
- Representing their respective domains or departments.
- Ensuring alignment with organizational goals.
- Mentoring Temporary Members and fostering collaboration.

### 2.2 Temporary Members
Temporary Members are invited to participate in the ARB on a case-by-case basis to provide expertise on specific topics or projects. Their involvement is time-limited and focused on:
- Contributing specialized knowledge.
- Supporting decision-making for specific initiatives.

### 2.3 Facilitator
The Facilitator, often a Project Manager or designated coordinator, ensures that ARB meetings are productive and that decisions are documented and communicated effectively.

---

## 3. Membership Types: Roles and Responsibilities

### Permanent Members
- Serve as long-term representatives of their domains.
- Provide strategic guidance and ensure continuity in architectural decisions.
- Nominate and mentor Temporary Members as needed.

### Temporary Members
- Participate in discussions relevant to their expertise.
- Provide input on specific projects or initiatives.
- Step down once their contributions are complete.

---

## 4. Membership Requirements

To ensure the ARB is composed of qualified and committed individuals, the following criteria apply:

- **Eligibility**: Members must hold an Architect-level role or equivalent within the organization.
- **Nomination**: Candidates must be nominated by their department Director and approved by the ARB Sponsors.
- **Commitment**: Members must dedicate sufficient time to actively participate in ARB activities and decisions.

---

## 5. ARB Size and Rotation

To maintain effectiveness and ensure diverse perspectives, the ARB is intentionally kept small, with:
- **Permanent Members**: Ideally 4–5 individuals, ensuring manageable discussions and decision-making.
- **Rotation**: Permanent Members may rotate periodically to provide opportunities for other qualified individuals while maintaining continuity.
- **Continuity**: Overlapping terms for Permanent Members ensure that institutional knowledge is preserved.

---

This revised structure ensures that the ARB remains agile, inclusive, and aligned with the organization’s evolving needs.
